## 1. Motivation

A fair comparison of continual-learning (CL) architectures requires not only matched parameter budgets but also an understanding of the **computational cost** each architecture incurs. Two models with identical parameter counts can differ dramatically in FLOPs, wall-clock time, and memory footprint due to differences in inner-loop computations (e.g., DGD updates in HOPE), recurrent state maintenance (Titans NMM), or specialised kernels (GatedDeltaNet Triton). This document presents an **iso-FLOP analysis** across all seven architectures evaluated in the thesis, profiled under identical conditions.

---

## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Profiling Setup

All models were instantiated with their **experiment-matching hyperparameters** (same configurations used in the actual CL pipeline) and profiled on a single NVIDIA GPU using `torch.profiler` with CUDA activity tracing enabled. The procedure for each architecture:

1. **Instantiation** — model loaded in `float32`, set to training mode.
2. **Dummy inputs** — synthetic tensors matching the CL experiment shape: `[B=4, T×N, D] = [4, 2048, 1024]` where $T = 8$ timesteps, $N = 256$ patches, $D = 1024$ embedding dim; plus action and state tensors `[B, T, 2]`.
3. **Forward FLOPs** — measured via `torch.profiler` with `with_flops=True` during a `torch.no_grad()` forward pass.
4. **Training FLOPs** — measured via `torch.profiler` over a full forward + L1-loss + backward pass.
5. **Wall-clock timing** — 3 warmup iterations followed by 10 timed repetitions; mean reported.
6. **Peak VRAM** — recorded via `torch.cuda.max_memory_allocated()` after peak memory stats reset.
7. **CL pipeline extrapolation** — per-step cost scaled to actual batch sizes and the **model-specific training schedule** (see Section 2.3). Most models use 40 base epochs × 5,000 clips + 10 task epochs × 1,000 clips × 5 tasks; however, **AC-HOPE requires 65 base epochs** for convergence due to its deeper DGD inner-loop dynamics. Batch sizes also differ: AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid and GatedDeltaNet use batch size 8 (vs. 16 for all others) due to higher per-sample memory pressure. These differences are reflected in the "Est. CL Hours" and "Total Train FLOPs" columns.

### 2.3 Training Schedules

The CL pipeline extrapolation uses the model-specific schedules below. All models share the same dataset (5,000 base clips, 1,000 task clips, 5 CL tasks), but differ in **base training epochs** and **batch size**:

| Model | Base Epochs | Task Epochs | Batch Size | Total Steps |
|:---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| AC-ViT (baseline) | 40 | 10 × 5 | 16 | 15,625 |
| Titans MAC | 40 | 10 × 5 | 16 | 15,625 |
| AC-HOPE | **65** | 10 × 5 | 16 | **23,438** |
| AC-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph8) | 40 | 10 × 5 | 16 | 15,625 |
| AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph11) | 40 | 10 × 5 | **8** | 31,250 |
| GatedDeltaNet | 40 | 10 × 5 | **8** | 31,250 |
| Transformer++ | 40 | 10 × 5 | 16 | 15,625 |

> **Note on step counts:** Models with batch size 8 have 2× more steps than batch size 16 models, but process the same total number of samples — so total FLOPs are unaffected by batch size. AC-HOPE's 65 base epochs, however, represent a genuine **1.5× increase in total training samples** during the base phase (325,000 vs. 200,000 clip-presentations), which directly inflates both total FLOPs and wall-clock time.

### 2.4 Caveats

> **Note:** `torch.profiler` may undercount FLOPs for operations executed in custom CUDA/Triton kernels (e.g., GatedDeltaNet's fused linear-attention kernel) and higher-order gradient computations (HOPE DGD inner-loop, Titans NMM online update). Wall-clock time is therefore reported alongside FLOPs as a complementary and often more reliable measure of relative computational cost.

## 4. Analysis

### 4.1 Parameter Budget

All architectures fall within a **41.6M–50.5M trainable parameter** band (±17% around the 43.4M baseline). The two HOPE-based hybrids (Ph8: 48.7M, Ph11: 50.5M) are moderately larger due to Titan memory projections, CMS level parameters, and (for Ph11) the DNH meta-controller. Crucially, these additional parameters serve CL-specific functions (in-context adaptation, structural evolution) rather than expanding single-task representation capacity — as argued in the separate parameter justification document.

### 4.2 Per-Step FLOPs

**Forward pass.** Most architectures cluster tightly around 580–732 GFLOPs per forward step. The clear outlier is **Titans MAC at 1,807 GFLOPs** (2.53× baseline), attributable to the Neural Memory Module's online gradient-based update that effectively performs an inner optimisation loop during inference. The AC-HOPE-Hybrid achieves the lowest forward FLOPs (580 G, 0.81× baseline) thanks to the CMS hierarchy skipping updates at slower timescales.

**Training step (forward + backward).** The spread widens:

$$\text{FLOPs}_{\text{train}} \in [2{,}024\text{G},\; 3{,}822\text{G}]$$

- **AC-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph8)** is the most FLOP-efficient at 2,024 G (0.95× baseline).
- **GatedDeltaNet** and **Transformer++** match the baseline closely (~0.98–1.00×).
- **AC-HOPE** costs 1.22× due to the DGD inner-loop backward pass through deep Titan memories.
- **Titans MAC** is the most expensive at 1.79× because of its per-step NMM gradient computation.

### 4.3 Wall-Clock Time

Wall-clock behaviour diverges significantly from the FLOP ranking, revealing the impact of **memory-bound operations, kernel efficiency, and gradient graph complexity**:

| Rank | Model | Fwd+Bwd (ms) | Relative |
|:---:|:---|---:|---:|
| 1 | Transformer++ | 173.1 | 0.34× |
| 2 | Titans MAC | 259.0 | 0.50× |
| 3 | GatedDeltaNet | 313.8 | 0.61× |
| 4 | AC-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph8) | 334.4 | 0.65× |
| 5 | AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph11) | 424.0 | 0.82× |
| 6 | AC-ViT (baseline) | 515.2 | 1.00× |
| 7 | AC-HOPE | 995.4 | 1.93× |

Key observations:

- **Transformer++** is the fastest architecture overall (173 ms), benefiting from highly optimised Flash Attention kernels and a straightforward MHA + GatedMLP pipeline with no inner-loop computation.
- **Titans MAC** appears paradoxically fast (259 ms) despite having the highest FLOPs. This is because the NMM update, while FLOP-heavy, consists of dense matrix operations that map well to GPU tensor cores. The shallower architecture (4 layers vs. 24 for AC-ViT) also reduces sequential depth.
- **AC-ViT (baseline)** is relatively slow (515 ms) due to its 24-layer depth, which creates a long sequential chain through the backward pass.
- **AC-HOPE** is the slowest by a wide margin (995 ms, 1.93× baseline). The DGD inner loop requires higher-order gradient computation that cannot be parallelised across timesteps, creating a severe sequential bottleneck. Combined with its longer training schedule (**65 base epochs** vs. 40 for all other models — see Section 2.3), this compounds to an estimated **25.9 hours** for the full CL pipeline — roughly 3× the baseline and 8.6× the Transformer++. Of this overhead, approximately 1.93× comes from the per-step wall-clock penalty and an additional ~1.5× from the extended base training phase.

### 4.4 Memory Footprint

Peak VRAM ranges from **3.90 GiB** (Transformer++) to **6.56 GiB** (AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid). The latter's elevated memory is due to the DNH meta-controller's gradient buffers for structural evolution and the additional CMS level states per block. All architectures fit comfortably within a single consumer GPU (e.g., RTX 3090 with 24 GiB).

### 4.5 Total CL Pipeline Cost

When extrapolated to the full training schedule (base training + 5-task continual fine-tuning), the **total training FLOPs** fall into two groups:

**Budget-matched group** (within ±10% of baseline):
- AC-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph8): 1.265 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (0.95×)
- GatedDeltaNet: 1.306 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (0.98×)
- AC-ViT baseline: 1.336 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (1.00×)
- Transformer++: 1.340 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (1.00×)
- AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph11): 1.468 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (1.10×)

**Elevated-cost group** (>1.7× baseline):
- Titans MAC: 2.389 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (1.79×) — driven entirely by 1.79× higher per-step training FLOPs (same schedule as baseline)
- AC-HOPE: 2.449 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs (1.83×) — a **compound effect** of 1.22× higher per-step FLOPs **and** 1.5× more training steps from the extended 65-epoch base schedule

The distinction matters: Titans MAC's elevated cost is an inherent architectural property (per-step NMM gradient computation), whereas AC-HOPE's cost is partially a training recipe choice (more epochs needed for convergence). If AC-HOPE could converge in 40 epochs, its total cost would drop to ~1.22× baseline, placing it within the budget-matched group.

The five budget-matched architectures are suitable for an **approximate iso-FLOP comparison** of CL performance. Titans MAC and AC-HOPE operate at a ~1.8× computational premium, which should be disclosed when interpreting their CL metrics relative to the other models.

## 5. Implications for the Thesis

### 5.1 Fairness of Comparison

The five models in the budget-matched group (AC-ViT, AC-HOPE-Hybrid, AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid, GatedDeltaNet, Transformer++) operate within a **±10% total FLOP envelope** and a **±16% parameter envelope**. This constitutes a sufficiently controlled iso-FLOP / iso-parameter comparison for evaluating architectural contributions to continual learning, consistent with standard practice in the architecture-search literature.

### 5.2 AC-HOPE Cost Caveat

The pure HOPE architecture (Phase 7, no attention) incurs a **1.83× total FLOP overhead** and a **2.9× wall-clock penalty** relative to the baseline. This overhead has two distinct sources:

1. **Architectural cost** — 1.22× higher per-step training FLOPs due to the DGD inner-loop backward pass through deep Titan memories.
2. **Convergence cost** — 1.5× more base training epochs (65 vs. 40), required because the DGD inner-loop dynamics take longer to stabilise without the representational scaffolding that attention provides.

Any CL performance gains must be contextualised against this substantially higher computational investment. The hybrid variant (Ph8) successfully resolves both issues by integrating the HOPE mechanism into a shallower architecture with attention, bringing total cost below baseline **and** converging in the standard 40 epochs.

### 5.3 Wall-Clock vs. FLOP Disparity

The weak correlation between FLOPs and wall-clock time ($r \approx 0.3$ across models) underscores that FLOP counting alone is insufficient for practical cost assessment. We recommend reporting **both metrics** in the thesis, with wall-clock time as the primary practical cost indicator and FLOPs as the hardware-independent theoretical complement.

### 5.4 Recommended Thesis Statement

> *"We profile all architectures under identical conditions (same input shape, GPU, and precision) and report per-step FLOPs, wall-clock latency, peak memory, and extrapolated CL pipeline cost. Five of the seven architectures — AC-ViT, AC-HOPE-Hybrid, AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid, GatedDeltaNet, and Transformer++ — fall within a ±10% total-FLOP envelope (1.27–1.47 × 10¹⁷ FLOPs for the full CL pipeline), constituting an approximate iso-FLOP comparison. Titans MAC incurs ~1.8× higher total FLOPs due to per-step NMM gradient computations; AC-HOPE incurs a similar ~1.8× overhead compounded from 1.22× higher per-step FLOPs and 1.5× more base training epochs required for convergence (65 vs. 40). Training schedules and batch sizes are model-specific and reflect the configurations used in the actual experiments (see Table in Section 2.3)."*

## 6. Per-Timestep Cost Breakdown

Dividing the per-step training cost by $T = 8$ timesteps yields the per-timestep amortised cost:

| Model | ms / timestep | GFLOPs / timestep |
|:---|---:|---:|
| Transformer++ | 21.6 | 267.96 |
| Titans MAC | 32.4 | 477.76 |
| GatedDeltaNet | 39.2 | 261.21 |
| AC-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph8) | 41.8 | 252.96 |
| AC-DNH-HOPE-Hybrid (Ph11) | 53.0 | 293.68 |
| AC-ViT (baseline) | 64.4 | 267.15 |
| AC-HOPE-ViT | 124.4 | 326.46 |

This view confirms that the **per-timestep FLOP cost is remarkably uniform** across most architectures (253–327 GFLOPs/timestep), while the wall-clock cost varies by 5.8× — further evidence that kernel efficiency and sequential depth dominate practical training time.
